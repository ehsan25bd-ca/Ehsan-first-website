'Critical Depth' :Program DCRIT;

The critical depth, dc, occurs when the specific energy is a minimum. For a given specific energy (other than Ec), there are two possible depths of flow for a given Q.

The depth, d1, represents a shallow depth of flow with a high velocity and a steep slope. More of the energy is in the form of kinetic energy. Such a flow is known as rapid or supercritical flow.

The other depth, d2, represents a deeper slower depth of flow with a shallow slope. In this situation, more of the energy is gravitational potential energy. This type of flow is known as tranquil or subcritical flow.
